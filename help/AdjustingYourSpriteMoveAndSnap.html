Adjusting Your Sprite - Move And Snap
 
 
Oooohhh, the long and weary road travelled!

Now, at last, we can add a sprite to the map!... or can we? (just joking)
 
So, armed with the knowledge from the "adding your first sprite" sections here and here, its time to get it on, so to speak.
 
Lets start with an example.

I want my player to be able to stand on something. Something that isn't going to scratch, deface, disembowel, or kill him/her/it. (we need to include "it" here so as to not offend game characters of a neutral disposition).

Well then, what am I to do?

To me, this sounds like a piece of map geometry that we wany to add (player can stand on it, doesn't hurt him/her/it), etc.
OK, lets set it up!

First, with your nice, new map opened in giLE2D, do the following:
 
1. Make sure you have selected the "Media" tab in the giLE2D browser. Also make sure you have the "Available Sprites" tab selected. Look at the image below... its shows you.
 
 
2. Now, looking at the image above... and using the stock artwork supplied with giLE2D, I have found a sprite image that will stand in nicely for some sort of Map Geometry block (a platform, the ground, etc). It is called "dirtMid.png", and it is going to be the stuff that my player can stand and run/jump on!
 
3. But, before I put this sprite into my map, I am going to use the "Sprite Type To Create" and the "Create Sprite In Layer" dropdowns to pick my sprite type and layer respectively. I already created layers (which, if you are reading systematically, you'll know how to do... if not, the section is here), so I'm going to do this...
 
4. Go to the  "Sprite Type To Create" dropdown and select "1-Map Geometry". Then go to the "Create Sprite In Layer" and select your map geometry layer you created. In this case, its called "1001-Map Geometry".
 
5. See that nice "dirtMid.png" file in the Media Browser? double-click it and watch what happens...
 
 
6. Its IN THE MAP!!! Yeahhh! giLE2D will place the sprite in the map at pre-determined default coordinates. The default coordinates can be changed in the settings dialog (we'll look at those later).
 
7. Anyway, I'm excited now! Lets select the sprite by clicking on it and then we'll drag it around the map. Note that when you select the sprite, it shows that it is selected by the way it looks, with boxes appearing around its perimeter:
 
 
8. Now just drag it around... fun! Now try to save your map... you'll get this...
 
 
9. In order for giLE2d to truly understand a map, it needs you to define where the player is to start! Kinda makes sense. So, we'll go to the  "Sprite Type To Create" dropdown and select "9-Player Start". Then we'll go to the "Create Sprite In Layer" and select your player layer that you created. In this case, its called "1004-Player".
 
 
10. Now, we're gonna choose a cute little avatar to represent the player. I'm using the stock media supplied with giLE2D, so I'm gonna pick "300.png"
 

LOLZ!!!
 
11. Double-click on "300.png" and our player is now added to the map! Drag  him/her/it above the map geometry you added earlier to give him/her/it something to stand on, and now you can save your map (highly recommended at this point!).
 
 
12. Click the "Save Map" button. You will see a warning that you are about to overwrite the map file with your changes. If you are ready to do so, click "Yes, Overwrite Current File". Your map will then be saved.
 
 
You'll notice that when you are dragging a sprite, that when you let go, it will snap to the nearest logical point on the map. This too can be changed through the "Settings" menu (see here).
 
Well, that was a long time to mention the word "snap", to which this section alluded! But I know you were itching to add a sprite or two to your map!
 
Anyway, now you get the gist of how to go about adding stuff to your map. In the next section, I guess I should tell you about the Property Inspector, where you can customize everything on an individual sprite basis!
